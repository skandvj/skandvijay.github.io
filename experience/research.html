Impact of Ad-Blockers and Anti-Trackers on Consumer

Experience

As a Research Assistant at Carnegie Mellon University, I contributed to a study titled "The Effect of Ad-Blocking and Anti-Tracking on Consumer Behavior." This research aimed to analyze the economic impact of digital privacy tools on consumer online behaviors and purchasing outcomes.

My responsibilities included:

  • Recruiting and managing participants for a large-scale field experiment.
  • Utilizing custom-developed tools to collect data on browsing behaviors and online purchases.
  • Conducting data analysis to evaluate the effects of ad-blocking and anti-tracking on consumer engagement and purchasing decisions.

Skills and Learning

Throughout this research experience, I developed and honed several key skills:

  • Data Analysis: Proficient in analyzing large datasets to extract meaningful insights.
  • Technical Proficiency: Experienced in using specialized software tools for data collection and analysis.
  • Project Management: Effectively managed project timelines and participant communications.
  • Understanding of Consumer Behavior: Gained insights into how digital privacy tools influence consumer actions.

Vision

My vision is to leverage my background in data analysis and understanding of consumer behavior to develop AI-driven products that prioritize user privacy and enhance user experience. I aim to bridge the gap between technological innovation and ethical considerations, ensuring that advancements in AI serve the best interests of consumers.

About Professor Alessandro Acquisti

Professor Alessandro Acquisti

Professor Acquisti is the Trustees Professor of Information Technology and Public Policy at Carnegie Mellon University's Heinz College. His research combines economics, behavioral research, and data mining to investigate the role of privacy in a digital society. His studies have promoted the revival of the economics of privacy and advanced the application of behavioral economics to the understanding of consumer privacy valuations and decision-making.
